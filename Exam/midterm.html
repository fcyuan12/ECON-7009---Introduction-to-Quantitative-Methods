Question 1

Definition 1 (imply) Given two propositions \(P\) and \(Q\), the statement “If \(P\), then \(Q\)” is interpreted as the statement that if the proposition \(P\) is true, then the statement \(Q\) is also true. We denote this by \(P \Rightarrow Q\).

(Sundaram and others 1996, 316–17)

Question 2

Solution. If the proposition \(P\) involves a universal quantifier, then its negation involves an existential quantifier: to deny the truth of a universal statement requires us to find just one case where the statement fails.
