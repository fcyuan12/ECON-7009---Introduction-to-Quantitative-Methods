Question 9

Consider a production function \(F(K, N) \in C^2\) that satisfies \[\begin{align} F_K & \equiv \frac{\partial F(K, N)}{\partial K} > 0 \\ F_N & \equiv \frac{\partial F(K, N)}{\partial N} > 0 \\ F_{KN} & \equiv \frac{\partial^2 F(K, N)}{\partial K\partial N} > 0 \\ F_{KK} & \equiv \frac{\partial^2 F(K, N)}{\partial K\partial K} < 0 \\ F_{NN} & \equiv \frac{\partial^2 F(K, N)}{\partial N\partial N} < 0 \end{align}\] and \[\begin{align} F_{KK}F_{NN} - \left( F_{KN} \right)^2 > 0. \end{align}\] The rental rate of capital is \(r\) and the wage rate is \(w\). From undergraduate micro, you know that a profit-maximizing firm will choose \((K, N)\) such that \[\begin{align} F_K(K, N) & = r \\ F_N(K, N) & = w. \end{align}\]

  1. (6%) Show that you can implicitly define \(K\) and \(N\) as a function of \(r\) and \(w\) from the above first-order conditions.

  2. (12%) Is \(K\) strictly increasing or decreasing in \(w\)? Is \(N\) strictly decreasing or increasing in \(r\)? Give some economic explanations for your results.

Related Definition or Theorem

Theorem 7 (Implicit Function Theorem) Let \(A \subset \mathbb{R}^n\times \mathbb{R}^m\) be an open set, and let \(F: A \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^m\) be a function of class \(C^p\). That is, assume that \(F\) has \(p\) continuous derivatives, where \(p\) is an integer, \(p \geq 1\). Suppose \((x_0, y_0) \in A\) and \(F(x_0, y_0) = 0\). Form the determinant \[\begin{align} \Delta & = \begin{vmatrix} \frac{\partial F_1}{\partial y_1} & \ldots & \frac{\partial F_1}{\partial y_m} \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \frac{\partial F_m}{\partial y_1} & \ldots & \frac{\partial F_m}{\partial y_m} \end{vmatrix} \end{align}\] evaluated at \((x_0, y_0)\), where \(F = (F_1,\ldots,F_m)\). Assume that \(\Delta\neq 0\). Then, there are an open neighborhood \(U\subset \mathbb{R}^n\) of \(x_0\), an open neighborhood \(V\) of \(y_0\) in \(\mathbb{R}^m\), and a unique function \(f: U\rightarrow V\) such that \[\begin{align} F\left( x,f(x) \right) = 0 \end{align}\] for all \(x\in U\). Furthermore, \(f\) is of class \(C^p\).

(Marsden, Hoffman, and others 1993, 398 Theorem 7.2.1)

Corollary 1 In the implicit function theorem, the \(\partial f_j / \partial x_i\) are given by \[\begin{align} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x_1} & \ldots & \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x_n} \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \frac{\partial f_m}{\partial x_1} & \ldots & \frac{\partial f_m}{\partial x_n} \end{bmatrix} = - \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial F_1}{\partial y_1} & \ldots & \frac{\partial F_1}{\partial y_m} \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \frac{\partial F_m}{\partial y_1} & \ldots & \frac{\partial F_m}{\partial y_m} \end{bmatrix}^{-1} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial F_1}{\partial x_1} & \ldots & \frac{\partial F_1}{\partial x_n} \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \frac{\partial F_m}{\partial x_1} & \ldots & \frac{\partial F_m}{\partial x_n} \end{bmatrix} \end{align}\] where \(^{-1}\) denotes the inverse matrix.

(Marsden, Hoffman, and others 1993, 399 Corollary 7.2.2)

Solution (a)

(6%) By Theorem 7, consider \[\begin{align} n & = 2 \\ m & = 2 \\ p & = 2 \\ F & = (F_k - r, F_N - w) \\ x & = (r, w) \\ y & = (K, N) \\ f(x) & = (K, N). \end{align}\]

Solution (b)

(6%) By Corollary 1, \[\begin{align} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial K}{\partial r} & \frac{\partial K}{\partial w} \\ \frac{\partial N}{\partial r} & \frac{\partial N}{\partial w} \end{bmatrix} & = - \begin{bmatrix} F_{KK} & F_{KN} \\ F_{NK} & F_{NN} \end{bmatrix}^{-1} \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \\ & = \frac{1}{\Delta} \begin{bmatrix} F_{NN} & -F_{KN} \\ -F_{NK} & F_{KK} \end{bmatrix} \end{align}\] where \[\begin{align} \Delta & = \begin{vmatrix} F_{KK} & F_{KN} \\ F_{NK} & F_{NN} \end{vmatrix} > 0. \end{align}\]

(2%) \(F_{KN} > 0\) captures the complementarity between two inputs. More capital employed increases the marginal productivity of labor, and more labor hired increases the marginal productivity of capital.

(2%) We find \[\begin{align} \frac{\partial K}{\partial w} & = \frac{-F_{KN}}{\Delta} < 0. \end{align}\] That is, \(K\) is strictly decreasing in \(w\). It means that as a higher wage \(w\) makes the firm to reduce capital inputs \(K\), a reduction in capital inputs will decrease the marginal productivity of labor.

(2%) We find \[\begin{align} \frac{\partial N}{\partial r} & = \frac{-F_{NK}}{\Delta} < 0. \end{align}\] That is, \(N\) is strictly decreasing in \(r\). It means that as a higher rental price \(r\) makes the firm to reduce labors \(N\), a reduction in labors will decrease the marginal productivity of capital.
