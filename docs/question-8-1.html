Introduction to Quantitative Methods

Question 8

Consider the following utility maximization problem. A consumer maximizes
\[\begin{align} \max_{x_1, x_2} U(x_1,x_2) = x_1 + a \log (x_2), \end{align}\] where \(a\) is a positive constant number. The constraint set is \[\begin{align} D\left(p_1, p_2, I \right) & = \left\{ (x_1, x_2) \in \mathbb{R}^2 \mid p_1 x_1+ p_2 x_2 \leq I, x_1 \geq 0, x_2 \geq 0 \right\} \end{align}\] where \(p_1 > 0\) and \(p_2 > 0\). In class, we have checked that the solution to the problem exists and the constraint qualification holds for all possible cases of solutions, and the derivatives exist, so the solution must satisfy the Kuhn-Tucker first-order conditions. Now answer the following questions.

  1. (8%) Set up the Lagrangian function and write down all the first-order conditions of Kuhn-Tucker.

  2. (2%) Prove that for all \(x_1 > 0\) and \(x_2 =0\) cannot be the optimal solution. You only have to check the Inada condition.

  3. (10%) Show that \(x^*_1 = 0\) and \(x^*_2 = \frac{I}{p_2}\) if \(I \leq a p_1\), and that \(x^*_1 = \frac{I - ap_1}{p_1}\) and \(x^*_2 = \frac{a p_1}{p_2}\) if \(I - ap_1 > 0\).

Solution (a)

(8%) The Lagrangian function is \[\begin{align} \mathcal{L} & = x_1 + a \log x_2 + \lambda_1 x_1 + \lambda_2 x_2 + \lambda_3 \left( I - p_1x_1 - p_2x_2 \right). \end{align}\] The first-order conditions are as follows.

  • \(\mathcal{L}_{x_i} = 0\): \[\begin{align} \mathcal{L}_{x_1} & = 1 + \lambda_1 - \lambda_3 p_1 = 0 \\ \mathcal{L}_{x_2} & = \frac{a}{x_2} + \lambda_2 - \lambda_3 p_2 = 0 \end{align}\]

  • \(\mathcal{L}_{\lambda_i} \geq 0\): \[\begin{align} \mathcal{L}_{\lambda_1} & = x_1 \geq 0 \\ \mathcal{L}_{\lambda_2} & = x_2 \geq 0 \\ \mathcal{L}_{\lambda_3} & = I - p_1x_1 - p_2x_2 \geq 0 \end{align}\]

  • \(\lambda_i \geq 0\): \[\begin{align} \lambda_1 & \geq 0 \\ \lambda_2 & \geq 0 \\ \lambda_3 & \geq 0 \end{align}\]

  • \(\lambda_i \mathcal{L}_{\lambda_i} = 0\): \[\begin{align} \lambda_1 x_1 & = 0 \\ \lambda_2 x_2 & = 0 \\ \lambda_3 \left( I - p_1x_1 - p_2x_2 \right) & = 0 \end{align}\]

Solution (b)

(1%) \[\begin{align} \lim_{x_1\rightarrow 0} \frac{\partial U}{\partial x_1}(x_1, x_2) & = \lim_{x_1\rightarrow 0} 1 = 1 < \infty \end{align}\]

(1%) \[\begin{align} \lim_{x_2\rightarrow 0} \frac{\partial U}{\partial x_2}(x_1, x_2) & = \lim_{x_1\rightarrow 0} \frac{a}{x_2} = \infty \end{align}\]

Solution (c) i.

(5%) Consider \(h_E = \left\{ h_1, h_3 \right\}\).

By \(\lambda_1 \mathcal{L}_{\lambda_1} = 0\) and \(\lambda_1 \geq 0\), \[\begin{align} x_1 & = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \lambda_1 \geq 0. \end{align}\]

By \(\lambda_2 \mathcal{L}_{\lambda_2} = 0\) and \(\lambda_2 \geq 0\), \[\begin{align} x_2 & > 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \lambda_2 = 0 \end{align}\]

By \(\lambda_3 \mathcal{L}_{\lambda_3} = 0\) and \(\lambda_3 \geq 0\), \[\begin{align} I - p_1x_1 - p_2x_2 & = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \lambda_3 \geq 0 \end{align}\] and \[\begin{align} x_2 = \frac{I}{p_2}. \end{align}\]

By \(\mathcal{L}_{x_2} = 0\), \[\begin{align} \lambda_{3} & = \frac{a}{p_2x_2} = \frac{a}{I} > 0. \end{align}\]

By \(\mathcal{L}_{x_1} = 0\), \[\begin{align} \lambda_1 & = \lambda_3 p_1 - 1 \\ & = \frac{ap_1}{I} - 1 \geq 0. \end{align}\]

That is, \[\begin{align} I\leq ap_1. \end{align}\]

Solution (c) ii.

(5%) Consider \(h_E = \left\{ h_3 \right\}\).

By \(\lambda_1 \mathcal{L}_{\lambda_1} = 0\) and \(\lambda_1 \geq 0\), \[\begin{align} x_1 & > 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \lambda_1 = 0. \end{align}\]

By \(\lambda_2 \mathcal{L}_{\lambda_2} = 0\) and \(\lambda_2 \geq 0\), \[\begin{align} x_2 & > 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \lambda_2 = 0 \end{align}\]

By \(\lambda_3 \mathcal{L}_{\lambda_3} = 0\) and \(\lambda_3 \geq 0\), \[\begin{align} I - p_1x_1 - p_2x_2 & = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \lambda_3 \geq 0. \end{align}\]

By \(\mathcal{L}_{x_1} = 0\), \[\begin{align} \lambda_3 & = \frac{1}{p_1} > 0. \end{align}\]

By \(\mathcal{L}_{x_2} = 0\), \[\begin{align} x_2 & = \frac{a}{\lambda_3 p_2} \\ & = \frac{p_1}{p_2}a > 0. \end{align}\]

By \(\mathcal{L}_{\lambda_3}\), \[\begin{align} x_1 & = \frac{I - p_2x_2}{p_1} \\ & = \frac{I - p_1a}{p_1} > 0. \end{align}\]

That is, \[\begin{align} I - p_1a > 0. \end{align}\]
